A complete VR Game

Using Unity VR

This VR Game isn't necessarily a complete game its more of a place for me to learn how to do all the things you could do in VR. The videos below show everything that I learned with specific details in the following paragraphs. Not specifically shown is the use of Unity's new action based system for using VR input with multiple headset devices and covers movement. Also hand pressence that can be change to show your specific device controllers. There are other minor things not necessarily related to VR mechanics but still cool things one can add in a 3D game like camera mirrors, reflection probes, reseting targets, and cloth physics.

In the first video below the player can use either direct interaction grab or ray interaction grab (force pull) to pick up the balls and put them in the drawer which is opened and closed by interacting with the handle and opens to a point with a configurable joint. The door works similarly expect it uses a hinge limiting how far it opens. Grabing the orange ball showcases an offset grab that is kind of like using the force to keep the ball levitating in place. We move on to a ray intertion that previews the next box color on the list and a lever with hinge that permanently changes it to the next color when pulled down and then the previous when pulled up. A brief show of how teleporting works on teleportable surfaces (purple floor) with a preview blue particle system effect to show where you will be teleported to (push forward on stick while holding trigger then release or press button 1 to teleport). Quick bowling on frictionless surface and two handed climbing using XR Base Interactable and overriding it a bit. Finally a lever to teleport on top of the tower.

The second video shows the three available pockets or inventory snap zones. We start with the two at belt level which changes depending on the players hieght and location when climbing or crouching. Then theres a back snap zone that stays roughly behind your head. All these snap zones use the XR Socket Interactor. With the assault rifle we can see the use of two handed grab which inherits from XR Grab Interactable with a couple overrides. The trigger hand on the rifle can independently affect the pitch, roll, and yaw. When both the trigger and the guard are being held with both hands they collectively affect the pitch and yaw but only the trigger hand affects the roll. Both the cans and target use a Lerp Reset script (linear interpolation) with the exception that the target cubes are frozen on the X and Y axis. Guns shoot a sphere projectile and light saber turns on and off and does sword things both with cool sounds.

The third video starts off by showing interactions with basic UI elements (drop down box, slider, button) using ray interaction. A bit more on showing teleportation and how you can't teleport through walls. The hat wall is a bunch of XR Socket Interators that can hold anything. There is also a socket on your head that can only hold hats. Your pockets can hold weapons and not hats or balls. For the three panel mirrors the two mirrors on the side use cameras that change in height with your head and the center used a reflection probe. At the end of the video is a show of cloth physics being interacted with by the light saber and itself.
