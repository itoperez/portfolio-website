A complete VR Game

Using Unity VR

This VR Game isn't a complete game, it's more of a collection of all the things I have learned to do in VR. The videos below show you through everything with specific details of what is going on in the following three paragraphs corresponding of course to the three videos. Not visually shown is the use of Unity's new action based system that accepts VR input from multiple headset devices and also covers movement. Also not shown is a smart hand presence that can be changed to show your specific device controllers. There are other minor things shown not necessarily related to VR mechanics but still cool things one can add in a 3D game like camera mirrors, reflection probes, lerp resetting targets, and cloth physics.

In the first video below the player can use either direct interaction grab or ray interaction grab (force pull) to pick up the balls and put them in the drawer. Said drawer is opened and closed by interacting with the handle and comes out to a limit point using a configurable joint. The door works similarly except it uses a hinge to limit how far it opens. Grabbing the orange ball showcases an offset grab that's kind of like using the force from a distance and keeps the ball levitating in place. We move on to see a ray interaction that previews the next box color on the list and a lever with a hinge that permanently changes it to that next color when pulled down. Pulling it up will change it back to the previous color. A brief show of how teleportation works on teleportable surfaces (purple floor) with a visual blue particle system effect preview to show you where you will be teleported to (push forward on stick while holding trigger then release or press button 1 to teleport). A bowling mini game on a frictionless surface and two handed climbing using XR Base Interactable and overriding it a bit. Finally a lever to pull at the top of the climb to teleport you on top of the tower.

The second video shows the three available pockets or inventory snap zones. We start with the two at belt level which will change in height depending on the player's height or when crouching. They will also stay at belt height when climbing. Then there's a back snap zone that is located roughly behind your head. All these snap zones use the XR Socket Interactor. With the assault rifle we can see the use of two handed grab which inherits from XR Grab Interactable with a couple overrides. The trigger hand on the rifle can independently affect the pitch, roll, and yaw of the gun. When both the trigger and the guard areas are being held with both hands they collectively affect the pitch and yaw but only the trigger hand can affect the roll. Both the cans and target use a Lerp Reset script (linear interpolation) with the exception that the target cubes are frozen on the X and Y axis. Guns shoot a sphere projectile. The light saber turns on and off and does sword things. Both weapons come with cool sounds.

The third video starts off by showing interactions with basic UI elements (drop down box, slider, and button) using ray interaction. A bit more on showing teleportation and how you can't teleport through walls. The hat wall is a bunch of XR Socket Interactors that can actually hold anything, not just hats. There is also a socket on your head but that can only hold hats. Your pockets can only hold weapons, not hats or balls. For the three panel mirrors the two mirrors on the sides use cameras that change in height with your head and project what they see onto the mirror. The mirror in the middle uses a single reflection probe. At the end of the video is a show of cloth physics being interacted with by the light saber and itself.
