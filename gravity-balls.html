The title Gravity Balls

A short subtitle

Gravity Balls is named that because I couldn't think of anything better. In this puzzle platformer the point of the game is to get the correct ball with specific personal gravity (green balls fall toward green edges) into the correct color coded pocket. This game however was not made with that wincase in mind but rather this was more of a project to explore possible mechanics in a gravity themed game. Move around with WASD or arrow keys and jump using spacebar, press again while in the air to do a double jump. Use the tab key to turn personal gravity on or off. You'll know it's on when you're glowing red. When you get close to another wall, left click to switch to that gravity. The gravity you switch to is based on the last up, left, or right collider the character has hit. In the case of multiple concurrent collisions you can decide where to pop to if you are holding that direction down otherwise the default is to gravitate to the last touch collider. Press the R key to switch between the following camera and static camera.

In addition to personal gravity the world gravity can be changed currently using 7890 on the keyboard but ideally the level designer will use this with additional obstacles that will fall accordingly for a balanced level. Personal and world gravity strength can also be altered. Objects and player will fall faster or slower depending of light, normal, or heavy gravity. The player also has extra abilities depending on gravity. Light gravity allows it to jump higher but not as far like Luigi and holding down the spacebar lets you hover. Heavy gravity is for a lower but further jump like Mario and holding down left shift triggers an accelerated smash. Normal gravity is just regular jump no abilities. Current though, just for you to get a feel for the game, all abilities are available from the start.

A couple additional things learned in this project include binary properties for determining the last touch collider. A homemade Cinemachine but then when I learned about Cinemachine I used it for a camera that follows the player and stays in the level bounds. More on Gizmos, forces, scene gravity, physical materials, and Unity's 3% extra collider area that kept getting my character stuck in tight spaces.
