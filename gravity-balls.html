The title Gravity Balls

A short subtitle

Gravity Balls is name that because I couldn't think of anything better. In this puzzle platformer the simple point of the game is to get the correct ball with specific personal gravity into the correct color coded pocket. This game however was not made with that wincase in mind but rather to explore possible player game mechanics in a gravity themed game. Move around with usual WASD or arrow keys and jump with space bar. Use TAB to turn personal gravity on or off. Once on jump or get close to another edge and Left Click to switch to that gravity. What gravity you switch to is based on the last Up, Left, or Right collidery our character is actively hiting. In the case of multiple collisions you can decide where to pop to if you are holding that direction down otherwise the default last touch will occur.

In addition to personal gravity the world gravity can be changed currently using 7890 on the keyboard but ideally the level designer will use this with additional obsticles that will fall accordingly. Personal and world gravity strength can also be altered. Objects and player will fall faster or slower depending of light, normal, or heavy gravity. The player also has extra abilities depending on gravity. Light gravity allows it to jump higher but not as far like Luigi and holding down spacebar lets you hover. Heavy gravity is for a lower but further jump like Mario and holding down the down key triggers an accelerated smash. Normal gravity is just regular jump no abilities. If

A couple additional things learned in this project include binary properties for determining the last touch collider. A homemade Cinemachine and then when I learned about Cinemachine the real one for a camera that follows the player and stays in bounds of level. More on Gizmos, forces, scene gravity, physical materials, and Unity's 3% extra collider area that kept getting my character stuck in tight spaces.
