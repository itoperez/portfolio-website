Blender Lava Lamp

A Blender Project

I wanted to solidify some of what I learn making the donut so I went ahead and made this lava lamp. This was more straight forward to model using a reference picture and artistically maybe not as hard as the donut or other organic things but still had it's own challeges. Really enjoyed this one as well with its blue and orange Rocket League, no wait, with its complimentary colors.

I started with a circle because that makes sense and extruded out the basic hollow shap. Then the middle section was separated and made thicker into glass and the remaining upper and lower section was made metalic. Now for the lava part a lot of trial and error was used to get the correct size and viscosity and it was made using metaballs. Metaballs have a weight and there is no other liquid in this lamp so to sell the effect of heating up the lava or plasma to make it rise I used wind. This included two wind turbines with the one below the metaballs (heating area) being stronger than one above (where the lava would cool and fall slowly). To futher sell realism two turbulant areas were added. The metaballs' orgin is used to collide with a wireframe invisible bounds to keep them from cliping outside the glass. The whole effect can be seen in slide two. I used the the same ideas from the donut tutorial to set up lighting, rendering, and make the final video. Set up in slide three.
