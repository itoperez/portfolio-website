Blender Lava Lamp

A Blender Project

I wanted to solidify some of what I learned making the donut so I went ahead and made this lava lamp. This was more straightforward to model using a reference picture. Artistically it maybe wasn't not as hard as the donut or as I imagine other organic things would be but it still had its own challenges. One of those challenges was figuring how to keep the lava in bounds of the glass and not floating away. Really enjoyed this project as well and I look forward to doing more.

I started with a circle because that makes sense and extruded out the basic hollow shape. Then the middle section was separated and made thicker into glass and the remaining upper and lower sections were made metalic. The lava was made using metaballs along with a lot of trial and error to get the correct size and viscosity. Metaballs have a weight and since there is no other liquid in this lamp to sell the effect of heating up the lava or plasma to make it rise I used wind. This included two wind turbines with one below the metaballs (heating area) being stronger than one above (where the lava would cool and start falling slowly). To further sell the illusion two turbulent areas were added. The metaball's origin point was then used to collide with invisible wireframe bounds to keep the lava from clipping outside the glass.
