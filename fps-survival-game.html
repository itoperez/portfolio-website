A FPS Survival Game

Using Unity 3D

FPS Survival Game is an open world ('open area' and with no boundaries so don't fall off) game with endless zombies/cannibals and wild boars trying to kill you. Try to survive as long as you can with your six given weapons [axe, revolver, shotgun, assault rifle, spear, and bow]. Switch between weapons using numbers 1-6 on keyboard or with the scroll wheel on your mouse. Enemies will spawn all over the map but will only chase and attack you when you are in their chase and then attack radius. Chase radius is made larger when enemies are hit. All weapons are shot by left clicking and can be aimed (a zoom in) with right click except the axe. The bow and spear require you to aim/draw before shooting.

This project required animating the player/weapons and enemies with an extra cool two camera trick with different FOVs to see the weapons and world. To deal damage the axe has an attack point attached to its head to detect collisions just like the punches and kicks in the Beat Em Up game. Spears and arrows have similar attack points attached on the head of the projectile. These prefabs that are instantiated when fired and shoot forward using a force. They also rotate to simulate gravity in a forward arch kind of way. The remaining gun weapons use Ray Tracing to register a hit. The player has a capsule collider that resizes when crouching and jumps with the player when jumping. Sound FX all around like usual and there is a scene manager.

Additionally there is a health UI and stamina UI which regenerates over time and is used to allow the player to sprint, located on the top left of the screen. Two more enemy UIs are on the top right of the screen. The top bar is for cannibals/zombies and the bottom one is for boars. They appear only if the enemy is actively aware of you. If you have more than one enemy after you, priority is given to the last one you have attacked. Finally a new enemy will spawn when another of its kind has been killed and will patrol the map on walkable terrain using Unity's NavMesh Pro.
