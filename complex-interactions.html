Complex Interactions

With Custom Hand Animations

Adding complex interactions in Unity VR is a great way to improve immersion. The three videos below showcase posible sinarios for using complex interactions. The first video shows custom hand animations on everyday objects along with complex interactions for the dial and slider. The second video adds actions to the complex interactions through supporting scripts. The last video combines it all together, complex interaction for teleportation with custom hand animation with VFX.

Custom hand animations are usually done outside of Unity, however all the animation and rigging for the hands in the first video were made in Unity with the help of a custom script using scriptable objects. The hand asset used has a skinned mesh required for these custom animations. In the second video I have created scripts for levers, sliders, buttons, switches, and a throttle all made with the idea to be reusable in any application.

The last video shows a unique way to teleport in VR for such cases where you want to keep both controllers as Direct Interactors. The teleporting wand was also my introduction to VFX and I built upon it in future projects like for flying in my Capstone project.
