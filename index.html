Game Development

I'm most confident in Unity and have done several projects in 2D, 3D, and VR. I'm most at home using C++, I've even taught it before, but with all the work I've done in Unity C# is close second. I've also worked in Unreal Engine but not as much and am comfortable using C++ and Blueprints. Although I am not trying to get an art related job, I thought it would be smart to learn the basics to be able to better work with artists in the future. With that in mind I learned a bit of Blender and have a decent understanding of it. I'm still not that great of an artist but its a start. I've also messed around in Aseprite a little usually to make smaller 2D assets.

Engineering

I have a Bachelor's degree in Engineering with a concentration in Computers. That means that I learned all about computer science but part of the curriculum included taking electrical engineering classes and low-level computer classes as well. In the first two years I also took civil and mechanical engineering classes so overall it was a well-rounded degree. In my final year I was part of Engineers Without Borders, an organization that does engineering humanitarian projects around the world. I also completed my senior project with a group of friends where my role was to create an Android app (see project below).

Business Management

After graduating college my brain hurt and I wanted to experiment with doing something a bit different and 'unsafe'. I decided that I would create an online retail business, Zoisite LLC. I did online arbitrage, wholesale, and private label on Amazon. After a few difficult months, I became profitable. A year later I was quite successful and confident enough in my business that I decided to consult others on how to set up and run their online stores. I have a website and storefront I can show. I learned a lot while having an LLC and feel that knowledge can be useful in the gaming industry as well.

My Work

Who I am

I'm Ito Perez based out of wherever I get a job.

I am a programmer that has loved video games my whole life and finally realized that making games was something I might like. I really love the blend between art and computer science needed when working in game development and am excited to be able to pull from my creative and analytical side while at work. Games are an incredible hands-on storytelling medium and I can't wait to be part of a team that shares the same sentiment. From what I've learned about myself I can confidently say that I am wired up for solving logical problems especially in coding. I have the ability to really dig into a problem to the point where I literally dream of nonsensical code solutions. They never work of course, but few things bring more joy than finally finding or creating that new solution that does.

On to the superficial side I like traveling, it's pretty cool, I even did a whole year abroad in Spain once that helped me get an International Studies degree. I've been to all continents except Oceania and Antarctica but I don't wanna go to Antarctica. Now I really enjoy humanitarian work. I've worked domestically with Dolly Parton's Imagination Library and internationally with Engineers Without Borders in Peru. If I ever get the chance I'd love to do anything like that again. I like to cook almost as much as I like to eat and was really into yoga for a while trying to get back into it. I like longboarding, snowboarding, used to play guitar sort of still do, and am nearsighted. Those are some of my more consistent hobbies but I also tend to have a bunch of new ones that come and go every couple months. I don't take myself too seriously but definitely take my work seriously and hope that is reflected when you see my work below. Alright that's all, thanks!
