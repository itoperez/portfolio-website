What I do

Game Development

I'm confident in Unity and have done several projects in 2D, 3D, and VR. I'm most at home using C++—I've even taught it before—but with all the work I've done in Unity, C# is close second. I've also worked in Unreal Engine but not as much, and am comfortable using it with C++ and Blueprints. Although I'm not trying to get an art related job, I thought it would be smart to learn the basics to be able to better work with artists in the future. With that in mind, I learned a bit of Blender and have a decent understanding of it. I'm still not that great of an artist, but its a start. I've also messed around in Aseprite a little, usually to make smaller 2D assets. Recently I completely a Professional Certificate in AR/VR Development and 3D Graphics from NYU.

Engineering

I have a Bachelor's degree in Engineering with a concentration in Computer Engineering. In addition to learning all about computer science during my degree, I also completed electrical engineering classes and low-level computer classes, as well as some civil and mechanical engineering classes in the first two years. In my final year I was part of Engineers Without Borders, an organization that does engineering humanitarian projects around the world. I also completed my senior project with a group of friends where my role was to create an Android app (see Thermoelectric Wrist Cuff project below).

Business Management

After graduating college, I wanted to experiment with doing something a bit different and 'unsafe'. I decided that I would create an online retail business: Zoisite LLC. I did online arbitrage, wholesale, and private label on Amazon. After a few difficult months, I became profitable. A year later I was quite successful and confident enough in my business that I began consulting work, teaching others how to set up and run their online stores. I learned a lot while having an LLC and feel that knowledge can be useful in the gaming industry as well.

Who I am

I'm Ito Perez, a gamer who builds games.

I am a programmer that has loved video games my whole life and finally realized that making games was something I might like. I really love the blend between art and computer science needed when working in game development, and am excited to be able to pull from my creative and analytical side while at work. Games are an incredible hands-on storytelling medium and I can't wait to be part of a team that shares the same sentiment. From what I've learned about myself, I can confidently say that I am wired up for solving logical problems, especially in coding. I have the ability to really dig into a problem to the point where I actually dream of nonsensical code solutions. They never work, of course, but few things bring more joy than finally creating that new solution that does.

On the personal side, I like to travel. I spent a year abroad in Spain while completing an International Studies degree, and I've been to all continents except Oceania and Antarctica (but I don't want to go to Antarctica). I also enjoy humanitarian work. I've worked domestically with Dolly Parton's Imagination Library and internationally with Engineers Without Borders in Peru. If I ever get the chance, I'd love to do humanitarian work again. I like to cook almost as much as I like to eat, I enjoy longboarding and snowboarding, and I play some guitar. On top of those, I also like to dabble in new hobbies every so often.
